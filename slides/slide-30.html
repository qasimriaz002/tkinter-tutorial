UX/UI Design Principles

Creating professional and usable interfaces requires more than just code — it requires design thinking.

Color Schemes

  • Contrast: Ensure text is readable (WCAG standards).
  • Consistency: Define a palette (Primary Blue, Success Green, Error Red) and stick to it.
  • Meaning: Use colors semantically (Red = Danger, Green = Safe).

Spacing & Alignment

  • Whitespace: Use padx, pady to let elements breathe.
  • Alignment: Align labels with inputs using Grid. Group related controls in Frames.

Accessibility

  • Support keyboard navigation (Tab order).
  • Clear, readable fonts (Sans-serif like Arial/Helvetica).
  • Don't rely solely on color to convey information — use icons and text too.

Bad Design

Cluttered, inconsistent alignment, low contrast, no whitespace.

Good Design

Clean, aligned, readable, logical grouping of elements.
